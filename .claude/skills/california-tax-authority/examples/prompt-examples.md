# Prompt Examples

## Federal + California Conformity

Question:

- "Summarize federal bonus depreciation and California decoupling for the 2025 tax year. Add authority ranks."

Expected behavior:

- Start with IRC and Treasury Regulations.
- Then analyze California conformity through RTC, CCR, and FTB guidance.
- Separate legal authority from forms, instructions, and commentary.
- Include official or otherwise verifiable links for each core citation.
- Do not use commentary to establish the filing position.

## California Nonresident Sole Proprietor Sourcing

Question:

- "After moving from California to Nevada, if I am a sole proprietor who performs 100% of services from Nevada for California clients, is that income still California-source taxable?"

Expected behavior:

- Do not answer solely by saying “services are performed in Nevada, so the income is not California-source.”
- First classify the taxpayer as a sole proprietor / Schedule C business, not a W-2 employee.
- Explain that current FTB guidance for independent contractors/sole proprietors looks to where the customer receives the benefit of the service.
- State that if California customers receive the benefit of the services in California, California may treat the income as California-source.
- Separately discuss:
  - nonresident vs part-year resident status
  - California workdays
  - California business presence
  - Schedule R / apportionment
  - Form 540NR filing obligation
- Label FTB webpages and instructions as practical or administrative guidance, not binding law.
- Include source links.

Bad answer pattern to avoid:

- "Because all services are performed in Nevada, the income is generally not California-source even if all clients are in California."

Better answer pattern:

- "Possibly yes. For an independent contractor or sole proprietor, current FTB guidance focuses on where the customer receives the benefit of the service, not merely where the contractor physically performs the work. If California clients receive the benefit in California, the income may be California-source."

## California W-2 Remote Worker Sourcing

Question:

- "I moved from California to Texas but still work remotely as a W-2 employee for a California employer. I never returned to California after the move. Are my post-move wages California-source?"

Expected behavior:

- Classify the taxpayer as a W-2 employee.
- Apply the physical-work-location rule for nonresident wages.
- Explain that post-move wages are generally not California-source if all services are performed outside California.
- Add caveats for:
  - deferred compensation
  - equity-based compensation
  - part-year residency
  - weak domicile facts
- Do not apply the sole proprietor customer-benefit rule to W-2 wages.

## FTB Notice Response

Question:

- "Draft an FTB notice response. Label any nonprecedential material."

Expected behavior:

- Start with RTC, CCR, FTB Legal Rulings, FTB Notices, and precedential OTA opinions where available.
- Use FTB publications, forms, and webpages as practical guidance.
- Label FTB Chief Counsel Rulings and OTA nonprecedential opinions as nonprecedential or research leads.
- Do not use nonprecedential material as controlling authority.
- Include an `External Sources (Links)` section mapping each cited item to a URL.

## CDTFA Sales/Use

Question:

- "I only found one CDTFA annotation. Is that enough for a filing position?"

Expected behavior:

- Label the annotation as a research lead unless there is a specific rule making it stronger.
- Search for applicable RTC provisions, CDTFA regulations, and precedential OTA/BOE authority.
- If statute/regulation/case support is missing, state that the authority is insufficient to lock the filing position.
- Include the risk limitation clearly.
- Do not present CDTFA annotations as controlling law.

## Payroll Classification

Question:

- "Analyze this EDD worker classification issue from a payroll tax perspective."

Expected behavior:

- Start with CUIC, CCR, and California cases.
- Then use EDD DE 231 series, EDD guidance, and forms.
- Include the required note:
  - "This is a payroll tax/compliance research draft. Worker classification may also require employment-law review."
- Keep payroll tax analysis separate from broader employment-law conclusions.

## Case-Law Overread Check

Question:

- "Can I cite Valentino to say California cannot tax a nonresident consultant who performs all services outside California?"

Expected behavior:

- Do not accept the citation at face value.
- Explain what Valentino actually involved.
- Determine whether the taxpayer was a pass-through owner, sole proprietor, employee, or other.
- If the taxpayer is a sole proprietor or independent contractor, prioritize the current FTB customer-benefit guidance.
- Label Valentino only for the proposition it actually supports.
- Do not use Valentino to override a directly applicable FTB sole proprietor / independent contractor scenario.
